Ozone in U.S. National Parks

U.S. National Parks

The green areas on the map are national parks and seashores maintained by the U.S. National Parks Service.

Ozone Exceedance

The bubbles represent the number of days in 2017 where a park has reached a level of ozone that the EPA delcares to be unhealthy for humans. Some parks don't have bubbles, either because they didn't exceed the ozone standard or because they don't have monitoring stations.

Nonattainment areas

The grey areas on the map are nonattainment areas, which are counties that the EPA classifies as consistently failing to meet air quality standards. Most air pollution in national parks comes from outside park boundaries. Parks by large urban areas tend to have greater air pollution.
